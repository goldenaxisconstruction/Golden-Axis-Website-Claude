OVERVIEW

An ADU is the best investment on your lot — if it's built right

San Jose homeowners build ADUs for rental income, family housing, and long-term property value. The difference between a smooth project and a two-year headache is almost always planning: setbacks, utilities, design, and permits resolved before construction starts.

Golden Axis manages the entire path: we evaluate what your lot allows under California ADU law, design the unit in 3D so you can see it before you commit, handle the permit process with the City of San José, and build with a fixed scope and milestone schedule. LOCAL KNOWLEDGE

Building an ADU in San Jose

San Jose processes ADU permits through its Planning, Building and Code Enforcement department, and state law requires cities to act on complete ADU applications within set timelines. Many San Jose lots — especially in Willow Glen, Cambrian, Berryessa, and West San Jose — have the space for a detached unit. We confirm utility capacity, sewer connection, and fire-access requirements up front, because those are the items that stall unprepared projects.

QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an ADU cost in San Jose?

Most quality ADUs land between roughly $250,000 and $500,000+ depending on size, site conditions, utilities, and finish level. Garage conversions cost less; detached two-bedroom units cost more. We give you a fixed-scope proposal, not a per-square-foot guess.

How long does the whole process take?

Typically 3–6 months for design and permits, then 5–9 months of construction depending on size and site. We give you a real schedule with your proposal and update it weekly.

Can you handle the permits?

Yes — permit coordination is part of our service. We prepare the plans, manage submittals and corrections, and schedule the inspections.

Do I need to hire an architect separately?

Usually no. Our design-build approach covers ADU design in-house, with licensed engineering brought in as required.
