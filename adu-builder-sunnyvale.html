LOCAL KNOWLEDGE

Building an ADU in Sunnyvale

Sunnyvale's One-Stop Permit Center reviews ADU applications, and California state law limits how much cities can restrict ADUs on single-family lots. We verify setbacks, height limits, utility routing, and tree constraints during the feasibility visit, so the design we show you is one the city will actually approve. Neighborhoods like Ortega, Ponderosa, and Birdland regularly support detached units of 600–1,000 square feet.

QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of ADUs do you build?

Detached new-construction units, attached additions, and garage conversions — plus junior ADUs (JADUs) where the lot supports them.

What does a Sunnyvale ADU cost?

Roughly $250,000–$500,000+ for detached units depending on size, utilities, and finishes; conversions run less. You'll get a fixed-scope number before you sign anything.

Will an ADU raise my property taxes on the whole house?

The ADU is assessed as new construction and added to your existing assessment — your current home's base value isn't reassessed. Confirm specifics with the Santa Clara County Assessor; we're builders, not tax advisors.

Do you handle everything, or do I need other contractors?

One contract, one team. Design, engineering coordination, permits, construction, and finishes are all managed by Golden Axis.
